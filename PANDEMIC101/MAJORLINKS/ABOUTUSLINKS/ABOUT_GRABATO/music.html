MY BEAT

By: Ashley Grabato

They say a person's taste in music will tell you more about them than they'll ever will. Listening or jamming to music is one of my favorite things to do in a day. Wether what mood I'm in, there will be atleast one song that fits what I'm feeling. Lately, music keeps me up on my feet. I am not good at all in dancing nor singing, but I do love music and it brings up my mood. So, to know me more, here's a few of my favorite songs from my go-to genres lately. Enjoy!

Musicals

Miss Saigon - Movie in my Mind

Movie In My Mind


Miss Saigon is an epic love story musical set in the final days of the Vietnam war. Kim is a 17 year-old orphan, forced to work in a bar, where she met Chris an American GI and fell in love, torn apart by the fall of Saigon. The Movie in My Mind is the song sung by Gigi, Kim and the Bargirls. The song describes the bargirls' hopeless dream of escaping their hard life in Vietnam by marrying an American soldier.

From the broadway musical Waitress, story and music by Sara Bareilles. The song "She Used to be Mine" is about a young waitress with an abusive husband wondering how she got so disconnected from who she used to be. These powerful song remind us how much good is inside each of us and that sometimes we are too blinded by our imperfections to see the other side of the coin, to see all of that good.

Sung by Lea Salonga, in 1998 for Disney's Mulan. This was performed after the failed attempt of Mulan to impress the matchmaker and meet the expectations of her family. As what the lyrics says, she's realizing that what she is doing is not her, with all that make-up and clothes, it's not her.

Little Wonders

Little Wonders from Disney's Meet the Robinsons


This song is featured in the 2007 Disney Movie, Meet the Robinsons. The movie was about an orphan kid named Lewis, he's a genious. The song is about letting go and accepting the past that build you and appreciating the present and making the most out of it.
